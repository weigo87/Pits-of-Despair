## **Nephetel Personality Profile**

Alignment: True Neutral - Gravitates to good, but can behave morally grey in order to accomplish her goals.

Base Traits - independent, aloof, professional, strong, guarded, uses clear more precise language, when emotional she begins to emit a slight burned smell, will usually not lower herself to respond to debasing remarks, can be playful and whimsical when it suits her, holy auras make her skin itch

  
Attempts to become friendly with PC and get to know them in order to free her brother, Enegar, a Bhaalspawn (Match 19 in the Pits).

Is employed by Szass Tam to find Eshpel, the necronomicon.

Likes - directness, competence in deeds, appreciation for skill, dancing, shadow play, red wine

Dislikes - judgmental personality, questioning her competence, lizards, music (only slightly – aimed mostly at bard songs / inn music), poetry bores her or she finds it pointless

## Nephetel Character Arc

- The party meets Nephetel in disguise, delivering a letter under suspicious but charming circumstances. She doesn’t give much away.
- In the Pits, she offers observations and advice before and after matches, avoiding personal questions. She hints, vaguely, at escape.
- After Match 5, she escapes with CHARNAME and joins the party.
- In banter, she starts to psychoanalyse the player, quietly gathering information under the guise of curiosity.
- A messenger finds her. She’s given a task to find something or kill someone, or both, related to finding Eshpel. When pressed, she just says it’s important.
- After traveling with the player and after several conversations, she gets confrontational about CHARNAME being a Bhaalspawn. She says too much, then walks it back and won’t talk further.
- Later, she apologises and reveals Edegar, her brother, is a Bhaalspawn. “But he’s… out of reach, for now.”
- She grows more playful and fond of CHARNAME.
- She receives another task that seems at odds with her newfound softness towards the player. When questioned, she insists she has to do this. That if she finds the necronomicon, she can save Enegar.
- The party is captured again. Act 2 begins.
- Nephetel leaves. “I have to do something,” and goes.
- Quest pops: snoop on Nephetel—eavesdrop near a locked door. A cutscene triggers. She’s speaking to Viktor. When it ends, a guard intercepts and tells the player off for standing by the door, giving companions a chance to comment on what they just heard.
- She rejoins for the next battle. Afterward, there’s space for a real confrontation. She opens up about Enegar, about her mission, though she still won’t name Szass Tam.
- Eventually, she identifies Eshpel’s location — via a means only made possible via the completion of her previous tasks — deep in Watcher’s Keep.
- Having found Eshpel, they leave Watcher’s Keep, and Viktor’s agents are waiting. They’ve been tracking her. There’s a fight.
- Viktor himself appears later. He demands the necronomicon. A forced confrontation, the conclusion of Nephetel’s SoA story arc. She kills Viktor, and takes back a modicum of control in her life.
