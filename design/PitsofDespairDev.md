## Introduction

Pits of Despair is a content and companion mod. It offers the player the choice of having a 7th companion in the form of an item, Esphel the Necronomicon. The content is surrounding the reaction of the world in Shadows of Amn and Throne of Bhaal to having Esphel. It will provide new areas, new encounters, new items, spells and powers. A villain to rival Irenicus and will extend the gameplay of Throne of Bhaal. This mod is a companion to the 1st Part with the Black Pits 2 mod and uses the Black Pits 2 for content as well as progression of the story.

        This story, at its core, is a companion story of an autistic individual with mental health issues from the struggles presented by deep loneliness as shown through an empathetic link between Eshpel and the PC. There is no romance but it does offer the chance of a relationship that can end in either a friendship or the more severe ownership path or some level in between.

It presents elements that show an understanding of an individual, while an object, who has faced trauma but has an innocence and how the player will help or hinder such a unique creature. The mod itself takes the player’s journey through Throne of Bhaal threaded by Esphels personal story and incorporates how BG2 will react to it. The Bhaalspawn story is an epic tale and this mod seeks to weave a lore-based classic adventure into it with a compelling companion.

## Mod Goal

        Pits of Despair is intended to bring joy to the players of IE games. That is the primary focus as well as motivation for putting in the effort and time to create it by all team members. How to do that is up for debate but this mod includes a defined purpose in how to do that.

1. Provide a satisfying storyline that progresses through mid-SoA to the end of ToB. This storyline while having both light and fun moments it will cross over to serious and dark tones at times. It is a story about characters who must deal with challenges associated with mental health, autism, social isolation and loneliness.
2. Memorable characters who are worth emotional investment and motivation. This will be accomplished through a combination of high-quality voice artistry, dialog, art, editing, interactivity and adaptability.
3. Allow for multiple ways to address situations that arise. Deviating from simple alignment choices to human choices. Your choices should matter and there should be something unique about that choice. While we endeavor to accomplish this everyone is different and something that works for one may not work for another.
4. No sexual, political, or hate content. Content creators of all types out there exist and each person has content they enjoy, those looking for those things will not find them here. There are friendships, relationships, innuendo and even salacious situations but it will never cross those very clear lines.
5. A strong foundation of fantasy D&D enjoyment. This means dungeons and even dragons. Challenging combat scenarios and characters. Variety of situations on how they occur and not simply spawning a pack of enemies in front of you every encounter.
6. Reactivity not only within the mod itself but integrated into the core BG2 storyline and sub-plots. The intent is to weave the content of this mod seamlessly into the campaign through organic and natural elements and integrations with NPCs, previous decisions, even equipment and if all things go well, meshing cross-mod compatibility.
7. New enemies, items, and spells that feel worthy and rewarding. Technically useful as well as thematically appropriate. Use of underused animations, sound, characters found within the game or were unsatisfying.
8. Compatibility with mods that provide or support a more enjoyable playthrough. While we cannot guarantee your favorite mod will make the list we will be making sure those that we enjoy will and provide a mod that is bug-free as possible.
9. Structure compatible with the Difficulty slider so that players of all skill levels may enjoy the mod.
10. To be consistent. This is very important but especially so with tone and the dialog., Even if there are writers for different portions of the mod the style should be consistent for the entire mod, with the goal of being consistent with BG2. This means writing styles that diverge will need to be edited to fit the style. We will seek a skilled or professional editor to that end as having disjointed writing styles and ones that break immersion. To put it bluntly, writing is very important.

# The Story Stages Overview

The Start: The Pits of Despair

In the Black Pits 2 starting with completing the last Tier 1 encounter Dennaton will present his normal lesser rewards but also a “A page from powerful magic grimoire”. These pages are a part of a “Eshpel’s Bundle of X Pages” series required for Eshpel to become complete. Once a second and all subsequent pages are acquired the PC will notice a peculiar effect where the pages inch towards each other and mesh together, while the writing may be disjointed it is eventually forming the bundle they are assigned to and tells. Once Eshpel is found the pages will escape from the character holding them and attach themselves into Eshpel which will give a contented sigh. Each Bundle, when returned, will cause Eshpel to present options to the PC for a particular power based on the relationship path conditions. #The power could be offered to another NPC at the Player’s discretion.

The area is under powerful wards that do not allow divination magic to penetrate to avoid both Tam and the Captain from attacking and taking the pages. While Eshpel may not have been encountered yet this provides narrative breadcrumbs to introduce it slowly if the Player opts to do BP2 when level appropriate. The Black Pits 2 pages are all a part of 3 total bundles, meaning all of T2, T3, and T4 must be completed before gaining the Bundle, allowing Eshpel to offer another power, and driving the core storyline forward. This is a required path as the PC must use the BP2 map to enter Thay and is the area where the “You will not Pass” Red Wizard Edition plot encounter takes place.

Small narrative additions will be placed in the BP2 areas as the PC advances including: A spying member of Szass Tam’s Cabal as well as agents from Caelar Argent’s faction

Prologue: Part 1 - The Starbreaker’s comeuppance

The Spelljammer ship, a Great Bombard named the Starbreaker, was raiding a prime material world in a distant crystal sphere when they were attacked by a rival ship commanded by a devious pirate captain intent on destroying them for past grievances and claim the prize of her bulging loot. The Starbreaker narrowly escaped its rivals to the phlogiston between spheres when she was attacked by a Queen and her Asteroid Spiders from a nearby unknown enormous nest. Again the Starbreaker was in dire straits.

Still being pursued by and the Starbreaker nearly crippled by the Queen, Captain Bohgil chose an act of desperation he had never considered before. He ordered the cataclysmic artifact known as Eshpel that had been fused to the base of the mast of the Starbreaker as a power source to have the ancient Netheril seals upon it broken. The Captain then used the awesome magic at his disposal to supercharge and push the Starbreaker a great distance from its pursuers to escape. What he had not accounted for was that Eshpel had been freed of its prison and with a scream teleported the damaged ship and crew across galactic distances. Free and fueled by its own hatred and the desperate chaotic emotions of those aboard the Starbreaker, Eshpel instinctively turned to Faerun, its long lost but never forgotten home. With the Netheril seals binding its strength removed the full might of its considerable magic to rip itself from the mooring of the mast. The mast shattered along the entire length, crippling the ship and throwing crew, supplies and pilfered treasure into the atmosphere of Faerun at breathtaking speed.The ship was rocked by explosions becoming more severely damaged with a massive hole torn in the side of the hull.

Eshpel itself was nearly destroyed, its binding split and frayed, flesh pages exploded into a shower of confetti and were sucked out of into the air. Chunks were torn from the covers where its sentient soul resided and sticky black fluid pooled on the floor. The Starbreaker screamed through the air spinning and twisting, its crew hanging on for dear life as it plummeted downward until eventually slamming into the Surmarsh in Thay.

### Nephetel Intro

A timer for 1-5 game days later will be set after the first “A page from a powerful magic grimoire” is obtained by the PC. After expiring and entering any Athkatla city map the PC will be approached by a member of the Shadow Thieves telling them that someone wants to see them and is offering powerful magic or a mountain of gold just to see them in Government District, near one of the fountains. #Before Chapter 3 use Imoen’s escape or information on her location as a lure.

When the PC visits the found the find a Shadowdancer who introduces themselves as Nephetel. She offers to purchase the page for a hefty sum with the promise of more if they happen to come across any others. If pressed about why she says she is a trader in exotic magic and has a standing order from a powerful mage about such magical pages. Unknown to the Player at that time she is a high ranking member of Szass Tam’s Cabal and was directed specifically to Amn to gather the pages. She will not give up her employer and will sweeten the deal with more items or gold in the hopes of gaining the page. She will send messages each time a page is acquired offering scaling amounts of reward.

Prologue: Part 2 - Falling from Heaven, literally

The Captain and Sigant were rendered unable to perceive or act, a considerable feat for a Demilich while Eshpel was broken, crippled, and frightened but hopefully defiant. It was now only two covers held together with bits of flesh leather and a scattering of pages clinging to each cover. It enlisted the aid of the only two beings who have ever willingly acknowledged it to escape, Mouf the mimic and Willy the undead headless Ettin.

Mouf was able to drag itself to the shattered remains of Eshpel laying on the deck and gathering it in its mouth spit it through the spell blasted hold as far as it could. Landing near the mid-decks and Willy’s unconscious form. (do undead go unconscious? The Nameless One knows better) Together they lay among the blood and debris of their home. Having been silenced for centuries Eshpel was wailing internally, panicking at the thought of the devil Captain waking and again sinking it into the ship but was immediately giggling uncontrollably as it remembered the mast had been splintered. Eshpel attempted to wake Willy but only discordant animalistic noises came out. It reached out with the magic it still possessed and shook Willy violently while grunting, drooling ichor and a wave of light. Willy abruptly sat up, the torso turning to face Eshpel and the arms ending in fingerless stumps waved wildly as if frightened. Slowly he stood up, turned, and began limping toward Eshpel. After a few futile tries Willy managed to pull it up and into its arms holding it tightly against its chest, torso leaning as if asking what to do next.

Bride was unconscious near Willy but began to moan and stir. Eshpel was frantic to escape, guttural noises and magical flashes swirled around Willy which is all that was needed for him to make the decision to jump. As Eshpel and Willy escaped wind ripped through the exposed hull of the ship and its contents, where many of Eshpel’s pages were swirling and shifting until finally being blown out into the air. As fast as he could limp, Willy shabled toward the hole blasted in the hull and flung itself out, falling free of the ship then down through the atmosphere toward Faerun.

The fall was long and loud, with air roaring around them. They fell fast and landed hard smashing into the earth causing Eshpel’s to explode magical power creating a massive crater. Willy’s body was ripped apart and smashed into the stones of the Keep motionless. Eshpel observed its surroundings, testing its returned ability to speak and remembering from centuries past the formation of words. Magic from Eshpel seeped into Willy’s body, slowly healing him.

### Bride Intro

        Bride, quartermaster of the Starbreaker, has been tasked with finding Eshpel. Using her demonic favors to humans on Faerun (a world she has never personally visited) she has ascertained there is a powerful individual, one of the spawn of Bhaal that has been making a name for itself all of Amn. Intrigued, she has found and followed with charm and seduction in her human form. She is waiting outside the Black Pits watching the Cabal escape (through cutscene) in disguise. Once the Cabal escapes with the page after interjections she will approach the party. She will be affable and charming in her own pirate-like manner asking if the PC needs assistance. Eshpel will be silent while interacting with the disguised Bride and she offers to assist the party for some “jink” (reference to PST, some players may recognize it, she has a mix of normal and sigil-speak depending on how good the writers are at pulling it off). Whether the PC accepts or refuses she will be affable and friendly, even seductive with certain dialog options. If accepted she tells the PC she saw at least one suspicious onlooker that quickly disappeared, stating it is “prolly som’ guppy try’n to make some jink off’n some famous cutter” and will track them down for them. When pressed how she will she’ll give a sinister yet sultry smile and pull her dagger letting the PC “iff’n he doesn't give me a stick, Imma give ‘im one”. She quickly disappears towards the Docks and leaves a cold trail. .No dialog will mention Eshpel as a sense of desperate fear overwhelms the PC even considering it. This is the first instance of Eshpel’s emotional link with the PC being shown.

### Showdown with Sigant

Upon entering the crypt room the PC sees Willy who has shuffled into the center of the room backing away from the party. This will require a minimum threshold of XP otherwise the room will remain empty. Eshpel is ranting and raving at Willy to escape but suddenly goes quiet after noticing the PC. After initial dialog choices which give indicators of the non-verbal and non-responsiveness of both Willy and Eshpel suddenly a tremendously powerful magical effect hits the room and a portal opens. The Sigant floats through the portal discovering Willy and Eshpel, completely ignoring the PC. Sigant is triumphant, ordering Willy to return to the ship with it to the portal. Eshpel is silent as Willy refuses in his own special way causing the Sigant to become enraged. After demanding Willy give him Eshpel (how does a skull carry a book? telekinesis) and being refused he explodes into a hateful rant and at the end disintegrates Willy, showcasing just how unhinged he has become. Both Eshpel and the Sigant are shocked which causes Eshpel to vocally cry out for help and promise power to the PC. Sigant snarls at Eshpel and then for the first time notices the PC, initiating combat. The moment Sigant is an inch from defeat he retreats through his portal, escaping while insulting the PC and vowing that Eshpel would pay for its defiance alluding to the Captain. The portal will snap shut behind Sigant, not allowing the PC to follow. There will be flavor text that Watcher’s Keep has changed in some way that will give indications that another such portal cannot be opened by outsiders (Helm is pissed, the Demogorgon has noticed). #Lock the crypt door until Demogorgon is defeated?

The Cabal collects

After the PC has met with Eshpel and the first time they reenter BP2 a cutscene will play showing a new group of adventurers, members of Szass Tam’s Cabal, fighting the next scheduled fight in BP2 due to information from Nephetel. Eshpel will be horrified as they easily dispatch and are rewarded by Dennaton with one of its pages. It will lose control and start pleading, threatening and groveling first with the PC to kill the powerful Cabal members. The PC will react poorly to this and at the end of dialog force Eshpel into submissive silence which reminds it of trauma it had been inflicted with by the Captain. Luckily none in the BP2 had seen or heard Eshpel speak and it remained hidden.

Dennaton forbids fighting inside of the Pit unless they are official challenges while in his arena and so the PC is left powerless to engage with the Cabal members there. Options to attempt to follow, ambush, negotiate, or break Dennaton’s orders and attempt to kill the Cabal members are presented. Ultimately, no option works out and the Cabal escapes with the precious page. After escaping Eshpel becomes angry, abusive, wild and uncontrollable. Inflicting ailments on the party and having extremely negative banter.

Amkethran - The Pirates strike!

The ToB internal section Chapter 8 of Amkethran’s story is unchanged except with the introduction of rumors of groups of exotic adventurers that have been searching for something before it was sieged. These exotic adventurers are two or more separate search parties of the Pirates looking for Eshpel and its pages. One of the effects of the artifact level power of Eshpel is that it is unable to be divined directly or the one who claims or holds it. This has caused the Captain to have to look not only physically but also employ informants with his vast wealth through proxies to find it and the lost pages to restore the significant magic to power his ship.

One party is led by Doc, the other by Bride. A bundle of pages is held by Yaga-Shura whose army minions had found them, ascertained their magical power and delivered them to their general during his siege. Doc’s party has infiltrated Amkethran and meets them some time before Yaga-Shura’s fight. When Doc’s party finds the PC and Eshpel he is surprised and delighted thinking that they are easy pickings and hoping to earn a promotion or greater share for returning Eshpel. Eshpel, while hinting at irrational or unstable behavior, suddenly rides a roller-coaster of emotions and dialog concluding in the PC realizing it is terrified of returning to the Starbreaker.

Once combat starts and the PC is hit by an attack or spell a memory or cutscene shows abuse by Doc that echoes through the PC and party. Eshpel’s dialog when this happens shows the emotional impact and causes the PC to lose control and briefly transform into the Slayer before the PC deals with the empathic link and reverts. Once Doc’s party is defeated (the Captain will Wish his crewmates back to life so they are available in future encounters for narrative purposes) the consequences of being attacked are then dealt with including the PC being involuntarily transferred due to Eshpel. First instance of a moral choice that affects Eshpel’s relationship with PC.
