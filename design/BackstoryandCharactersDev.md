# The Backstory

(Does not exist in official Faerun lore)

Eshpel is a journal and spell book Necronomicon from the time of a cataclysm that created the Sea of Stars on Faerun, and the fall of Netheril. It became sentient in the flying city of Eileanar, the home of the legendary arcanist [](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Karsus&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776533510324744&usg=AOvVaw2ObGsmpvi9kw7kqJEj2cWU)[Karsus](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Karsus&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776533510324875&usg=AOvVaw1BO_HwLryIARG-WCS7uE7x). Karsus momentarily became a demigod to form his avatar and Eshpel became aware of its own existence. As the weave was destroyed, Eshpel absorbed some of Mystryl's essence blocking Karsus's access to the weave, suddenly removing the existence of magic from Faerun. As the floating city fell Eshpel was flung far to the east and was lost to the depths of the Sea of Stars and there it stayed for centuries, hidden under rubble and sand, unknown, alone, and lost. While magic was as natural as breathing underwater to those occasional fish brave enough to venture into the wreckage of the magical empire, they did not give Eshpel the chance even to understand the concept of speech, let alone how to accomplish it.

Eshpel couldn't affect its environment, move on its own power, or use its magic to affect the greater world. It was a spell book; it was meant to be used not to be a user. This may have been a side effect of Mystryl's sacrifice or perhaps the proximity to that world-changing event. Karsus was long gone and his creation was left to fend for itself in a world that did not know it existed.

Long years, then centuries passed and as they did Eshpel's sanity began to erode. How would any living being not? Trapped within its own mind for centuries where the horrors of an apocalypse existed and hallucinations that its powerful magic showed to it. Hallucinations of being whole, a living being that could move and interact as it had seen with the various creatures of the deep. That was until a Sahuagin who roamed close by was called by Eshpel's desperation to find it.

Finally! It was free of the silent tomb but soon realized its captor had no concept of its value or sentience. Many of the creatures of the sea communicated by movement and Eshpel couldn’t even do that. It marveled at the sounds that came from the fishman's lips but could understand none of it. The Sahuagin warrior returned to its home but was abruptly called by its high priestess to fight an invading pirate ship into their waters. Unwilling or unable to resist taking its prize with it the Sahuagin and the rest of his warband swam to the water line to attack the pirate, Captain Bohgil.

The Sahuagin were slaughtered by the pirates easily as they were far outclassed by the powerful Captain Bohgil and his crew. Eshpel was overjoyed to have finally found living beings to be able to relate to but could not form words or tell them it was a living being. Perhaps it was fate that it could not do so for the captain was a black-hearted humpback who raped, pillaged, and raided any ship or port with the exception of his employer, Szass Tam, the Zulkir of Necromancy. Tam had hired him to find or steal any magical items and he did so with deadly proficiency. Eshpel was a powerful artifact that could not be affected in any way by divination magic, even that which identified it as being magical at all. It was the construction of the Eshpel made of heavy gold and the ancient writing within made it clear to the pirates that it was valuable and they were greedy to collect.

                Shoved into an old treasure chest and stowed below within a secret cargo hatch was where Eshpel found itself. It could not see anything but it did start to hear sounds of human language and not only that but it started to understand those sounds as speech. It was like water to the man dying in the desert and he cherished every muffled sound the crew made.  To any normal being this would have been a harrowing experience as this was a rough pirate crew in close quarters, but to Eshpel it was a celestial choir.

Beginning to learn those sounds and speech a realization was quickly dawning on Eshpel, it was not like those that had rescued it from the depths not only in form but in thought. Its mind was fractured, suffocated, and divergent. Being alone for every instance of its “life” previous to hearing the crew interact made it realize the concept of being alone and how angry it was at being forced to live that way for centuries. A sudden bout of mania ensued and a powerful streak of magic burst from Eshpel hammering through the cargo hold and into the ship. So it was that a small piece of his power and personality was infused with the chest it inhabited and the seed of a treasure mimic and friend was planted.

Magic rocked the ship and the pirates quickly changed course to return to Thay, intent on keeping their sizable haul and repairing what damage had occurred when the burst almost capsized their vessel. Tam had always used and discarded adventurers throughout his career to find magic items and the captain was no exception. Bohgil intended to never let Tam have the chance to kill him and always treated the Zulkir carefully and with distrust.

Tam was not impressed when Bohgil attempted to sell him Eshpel. Its artifact-level defense against divination gave him little more than idle curiosity about it. When viewed, Eshpel's pages were a mix of ancient languages, gibbering nonsense, and garbled magical symbols making it appear almost worthless. This was one of the side effects of the trauma it had endured during the years beneath the seas. Bohgil and Tam were concluding their business and enjoying a cup of wine when the matter of a game of chance came up. It was a common pastime between the two when it came to the end of a deal. With a simple toss of the dice either Tam won Eshpel from Bohgil and their business was concluded.

Lady Tymora was not with Bohgil when Tam had won, like so many things a simple act of luck changed the course of both their fates. Bohgil left while letting loose a roaring round of pirate cursing and Tam took Eshpel.  He stored it deep in his vault where lesser items of value were held that were rarely visited. Once again Eshpel was trapped in a prison where those who knew he existed, Tam, and apprentices, ignored him. Tam thought of him as a trinket, only useful as currency with no magical potential.

Years crawled by and Eshpel passed the time learning what he could on how to speak, difficult and rare were the muffles in Tam's demesne that he could hear. That was until it was rediscovered as Tam was preparing to perform the ritual to transform into an undead lich. Remarkably, this book he thought was useless shimmered with indescribable power and potential.

It was then that Eshpel began to speak, which both surprised and concerned Tam. What had been left rotting in his storage for all these years when he desperately needed a source of knowledge to appear as if by providence? Tam was always suspicious but this was far too convenient. Eshpel tried to use what he knew to communicate with Tam but it was nearly impossible to decipher and Tam, while curious, was much more interested in what he could learn from the pages of the book and not what he could decipher from its garbled speech. The soon-to-be undead quickly became tired of the gibberish that the book would blather whenever it was in his presence and with a warding ritual, he learned from reading Eshpels own pages, he sealed its speech. Was fate mocking Eshpel? It was now the reason for its own dreaded silence.

Szass Tam was said to have become a lich due to being wounded horrifically after attempting to invade Rashamen and it was said he drank an elixir that transformed him into the lich. However, this was an intricate deception, Tam only needed a way to have taken a mortal wound and with that accomplished he had, in secret, discovered the secret of lichdom in the twisted pages of Eshpel.

Eshpel silently watched as the wounded Zulkir became a lich fueled by the spells and knowledge of its own pages. Not for the first time Eshpel experienced rage, hot and white straining against its bindings, but now had a name for it, Tam. All it could do was scream in silence as magic roared out of its pages and destroyed a host of Tam's apprentices and servants, wounding the freshly undead Tam in the process. This startled Tam and in his newfound form he quickly sequestered it away under guard and spell until he could discern what exactly this artifact was, its potential, and how he could control it.

While Tam was rising to power and becoming a master of the undead his old colleague Captain Bohgil was biding his time. The hunchback had been bitter about losing that one dice game to Tam and it gnawed at him for some reason. He had taken a blow to his ego, and secretly wondered if he could turn the tables on the lich and steal his horde out from underneath him while he was distracted with his new transformation. Over the years he had been able to break away from Tam and lived to tell the tale, a rare feat when the Zulkir so commonly murdered his former treasure gatherers.

Bohgil had concocted a plan to execute a raid on Tam's stockpile while he was weakened. A talented wizard with a highly trained but disposable crew, the plan was to breach the compound of Tam's lair and steal away with a large portion of the magical artifacts he found within. The plan was launched when Tam was away announcing his new form to the Zulkir’s of Thay. While enacting his plan, to his surprise, he found the Necronomicon set aside under heavy guard and powerful magical wards. He was curious as well as angered that his suspicions about the value of the book were accurate and he had been swindled. It is this distraction which nearly became his undoing. He and his crew were overwhelmed after taking Eshpel and a frantic escape was made, when it was over and he was returning to his ship he was the sole survivor of the heist, quickly fleeing on his hidden ship to the sea laughing bitterly at the price of his distraction and the book.

It took over a year for him to unravel the magic of the wards binding Eshpel and he was surprised that once undone the book could speak and did so like a gibbering lunatic. Try as he might, the captain could not decipher what Eshpel was trying to say as it was almost incapable of communicating cogently. Eshpel’s excited mind would far too quickly jump from one topic to the next, often halfway through a thought not understanding that it had not said anything in the meantime.

However, as the book was speaking Bohgil noticed that the text on the page he was reading suddenly began to shift and twist, phrases and sentences went from being unrecognizable to being pieces of magical spell craft he could understand. The ettin hunchback quickly began speaking to the book waiting for the runes and symbols to unravel and twist into words and numbers he could understand. His unique telepathic bond with his second head and “brother” Sigant allowed him to not only hold what amounted to a conversation with Eshpel while Bohgil deciphered the incantations, recipes and secrets.

Eshpel was unable to tell that Bohgil and Sigant were simply using him, he felt as if he was connecting to the ettin, not understanding the nuance of conversation. Eshpel’s stilted conversations with Bohgil would uncover incredible finds and powerful spells while at times it would be a simple journal of a man with constipation or a grocery list at an ancient bazaar. It was not long after discovering how to make Eshpel decode the contents of its paged before Bohgil and Sigant uncovered the same ritual to become for lichdom. They decided immediately to follow through with it, knowing the power that would come with the transformation.

Within a short time, the ettin wizard had become a lich. They revel in the power but were quickly becoming tired of Eshpel. It seemed as if the book was infecting it with its mental instability and Sigant was particularly susceptible at times adopting the same speech and Bohgil could, through their telepathic link, feel his mind changing. They turned cold to Eshpel, closing communication with it and what it did was distant and abusive.

Time passed and Eshpel soured on his relationship with the brothers. They often left it to sit for days in the ship's hold, hidden in a smuggler's stash, which hardened Eshpel towards them. Was it destined to forever be alone and neglected? Fear and anxiety consumed Bohgil and Sigant, the book was affecting him and it knew his secret.

No one knew he was an ettin and it would be considered laughable for many that one could even become a wizard let alone a powerful lich. He had always hidden his brother Sigant in the guise of a hunchback both physically and mentally but when unlocking Eshpel and its secrets he had made himself vulnerable to it blurting out their secret in one of its nonsensical rants. It had to be dealt with soon.

During his years of voyages and with his connections across the seas of Faerun there was a rumor, a rumor of ships that sailed on the sea of stars and it intrigued Captain Bohgil. With Szass Tam searching for him it became an obsession to find a way to captain one of these so-called [](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Spelljammer&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776533510343033&usg=AOvVaw01ocD83YqGbkMy_7iADlch)[Spelljammer](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Spelljammer&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776533510343172&usg=AOvVaw3ANvudFkcY6Sq3ROVe5jb6) and sail to worlds he had never dreamed of.

A desire for freedom infected Bohgil, he would call it simple pragmatism. Neither he nor Eshpel realized it was the link that had been forged between them that gave him such ambition. Bohgil would call it luck, but in fact it was the Eshpel’s unique magical power that suddenly gave him an opportunity. A path through the planes to [](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Sigil&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776533510343921&usg=AOvVaw2GNq6iDb-iUn5cZmd13eRk)[Sigil](https://www.google.com/url?q=https://forgottenrealms.fandom.com/wiki/Sigil&sa=D&source=editors&ust=1776533510344026&usg=AOvVaw05Jqazzdt0JWB3y9darLBU), the City of Doors was opened by a demonic contact at great expense. This was at the very least a place that Tam would have considerable trouble finding him within and seek a berth on a Spelljammer.

With Eshpel in tow he arrived in the Cage using a significant portion of his wealth and found arcane adepts that could seal its corrupting influence and focus the power it radiated, but it required sealing every aspect of Eshpel including its ability to communicate. While performing the incantations the arcane adepts sent a magical shockwave through the Sigil which no doubt the Lady of Pain did not appreciate.

Before punishment could be doled out by the denizens of Sigil, Bohgil took one of its many doors with the help of another like-minded demonic sailor, the Burning Bride, or Bride for short.  They joined the crew of the Starbreaker, captained by a githzerai by the name of Xhak.  Hours later they were sailing into the depths of the Astral Sea. From Limbo to Wildspace Bohgil and Bride sailed together on the Starbreaker, they were both quickly recognized for their strength and had a reputation for being deadly in battle.

Bohgil was the more politically savvy of the two and rose in rank above Bride, whether it was through assassination, luck, or Eshpel who he kept as close as his second head his cunning was recognized by Xhak. Deep in Wildspace fleeing a Gish fleet for making off with one of their cannon prototypes the Starbreaker entered phased space, home to a Time Horror. Those of the crew who did not have long lifespans withered to dust immediately while the captain, Bohgil and Bride fought passionately to breach the phase and escape.

The ship nearly collapsed in on itself as it was pulled free with Bohgil at the helm. In the end the entire crew with the exception of Bohgil, Bride, Xhak and the ship's gunner Jammi were lost. The Starbreaker was crippled and its Spelljammer helm was crumbling, with time running out and the Time Horror far too close. Bohgil quickly made it to the base of the ship and thrust Eshpel into the wood and metal of the base of the mast. The Starbreaker shook violently as Eshpel merged with the mast and one of the wards was manipulated to seal it with the ship.

Power radiated from Eshpel and surged through the ship, filling spaces that had been eroded away and the sail withered into dust. The helm began emitting pulses of scintillating lights and Captain Xhak took to the throne to guide the Starbreaker. As he did, Bohgil climbed up from amidships, his own body glowing with the same scintillating light and drew his cutlass. “Mutiny” cried Xhak as he remained glued to the helm by Eshpel’s magic. Xhak was quickly cut down by Bohgil and the lich claimed the Starbreaker for his own.

# The Characters

### Eshpel,  Artifact - Chaotic Neutral

This character will be represented through an item that the PC carries and can be placed in the quick-slot and provide castable powers. The PC will be able to read flavor text of Esphel’s history as provided in the backstory based on many pages and bundles of pages recovered. Esphel will also have the ability to create a few wizard spell scrolls tied to its progression.

The characters' voiced dialog tone ranges from reluctant and uncomfortable, to excited and innocent depending on the topic or character they are speaking to. It seems as if it is unfamiliar with how to speak by stopping to think how to say it, stuttering or slurring certain unfamiliar words, or understanding the proper social etiquette in conversation. Blunt while at times charming and affable. It has a rudimentary understanding of humor but craves the attention and positive associations that go with it. It is unafraid of social boundaries or new experiences as it craves them.

Eshpel is autistic and traumatized, having been both abandoned and enslaved without a voice for much of his existence. Its mannerisms are rough and unrefined, the understanding of social cues, hierarchy, and decorum are concepts it has no practical experience with and as such he often seems at odds with itself as well as the PC and party in regards to communication. The only stereotypical relationship mannerisms it seems to understand is with his “pet” mimic Mouf and he displays affection, understanding and care with the mimic which it finds difficult to find the same path with its newfound companions.

Raw magic is infused in the book and with it raw emotion. Very quickly an empathetic bond will develop between the PC and Eshpel as this is one of it’s talents born of need for a form of understanding with other sentient beings. This bond is 2-way and not only do the emotions and thoughts of Eshpel affect the PC but vise-versa. It is often unstable in trying to accomplish his wishes and will often submit to the will of i’s owner except under extreme distress.

Much of its existence it has been alone, lost under the waves of the sea or silenced, trapped in a prison melded to the Spelljammer ship, the Starbreaker. The only two “owners” of Eshpel have betrayed the trust and power that it has offered both, Szass Tam and Captain Bohgil. It carries the years and trauma well for what it has experienced and it has a fierce desire to find someone who will care for it and that it can care for in friendship as it is closely attuned to the joy of positive feelings.

Because of the dichotomous interactions of what it has experienced and what it desires it is often chaotic. While extremely intelligent it can get lost in its own thoughts and speak without knowing it is doing so or falling silent while it has a conversation in its own mind and says only portions of it outloud.

It has little understanding of morality but is very sensitive to positive and negative emotions. It feels guilt and shame where no reason exists because of its treatment throughout its sentience. It seeks validation, acceptance, understanding and friendship at a core level. It will have harsh judgment of experiences it is familiar with, like enslavement, loneliness or being used while things that are outside of them he has no compass with which to understand if an act is good, evil, lawful or chaotic, only selfish or altruistic.

When confronted with a character that chooses evil acts its reaction may be muted in cases where it is unfamiliar or accepting of verbal abuse or malefic acts. Other times it will be emotionally charged with rage or violence (inasmuch as it can cause it) to those things that are close or precious to it, like freedom, Mouf, and potentially the PC.

Stats: None

Mechanics 

Eshpel is not a traditional companion and is considered a 7th member of the party.

The Relationship Path of Eshpel

The relationship path of Eshpel is tied to how it is treated, spoken to, and spoken about and the actions of the player in relating to it, the ToB storyline, and the companions it has banters with. Once acquired, the relationship path starts a series of global values and triggers will track the progress. This will be a non-binary choice or cause and effect allowing some freedom for players to roleplay their desires without needing to game for the best in-game results or restricted only to alignment style decision making.

#Three values will be used; 1. Trust, 2. Emotional State, 3. Chaos These values will allow a finer grade to create thresholds for banter, abilities granted, and reactions to be able to provide variety and appropriateness based on the interactions with the in-game story, ToB, and banter.

## The Pirates of the Starbreaker

(Does not exist in official Faerun lore)

Captain Bohgil commands a very formidable Spelljamming crew that has worked for centuries together raiding, pillaging, and enslaving entire countries on worlds in Crystal Spheres across Wildspace and the Astral Sea. The whims of the captain fluctuate as he searches for stimulation in his eternal existence as if seeking a way to escape the drudgery of unlife but he is a consummate pirate captain and commands his ship in all pirate traditions.

The Starbreaker is a Starjammer class Giant Bombard, a sloop, which looks much like its seaborn cousins with a giant cannon on its fore deck and ballistae on each side. The primary mode of transportation of the pirates that sails through the phlogiston to the various Crystal Spheres to planets found within. During their travels, they have also sailed on the Astral Seas and deep within Wildspace. It has seen many repairs and wear from normal use but also being trapped in lost time for over 100 years. It is a mixture of traditional Starjammer along with unique, rare, and magical materials that Bohgil has accumulated in his centuries as its captain.

### Bohgil, Captain of the Starbreaker – Neutral Evil

Primary antagonist. Astral Demilich and primary skull of Ettin Demilich. Has telepathic connection to Sigant and can speak through it. Captain of the Starbreaker. While mortal he was secretly a mutated human/Ettin hybrid that possessed vast intelligence covering one of his heads with a custom robe or cloak appearing as if he was humpbacked. Formerly a slave in a long past and forgotten culture he eventually found his way to Netheril where he through luck or talent was able to begin learning magic, first Transmutation then as he aged Necromancy seeking ways to adapt his mutated form and then as he was aware of his limited lifespan that his genetics mandated to become a lich. Upon becoming a lich he broke away from his primarily solitary ways and decided on the very unlikely profession of becoming a smuggler, pirate, and privateer initially for Thay but eventually the entire world of Faerun.

He had an affinity for transforming and offered those of his crew who survived unique opportunities for long life to continue as his companions and over the years he sailed not only the seas of Faerun but eventually the planes, the Astral Sea, and eventually Wildspace. Calm, wickedly intelligent, becoming a demilich has made him lazy but he still retains the incredible cunning he possessed to achieve something no other Ettin has. His ship is his prized possession and he is very protective of it and desperate to get off the hateful land of his birth and wander free. He has hidden his second head from the world and even his crew for hundreds of years due to his deeply ingrained embarrassment for his heritage. Explaining away the second demilich skull as a servant, summoning or any number of other plausible excuses until after years he replaced Sigant with himself in order to quell a mutiny. When defeated along with Sigant after having been absorbed, Bohgil will merge with Sigant and become a single entity, a Ascended Astral Demilich.

The captain, Bohgil, was a lich with a horrible secret. He was actually a human-sized Ettin. To survive he had covered his rightmost head with clothing claiming he had a hump from a young age and hid any hint of it in the presence of others. This allowed him to do things that no Ettin ever had, if they had the base intelligence to do so, and become a wizard. He learned magic, all the while his brother Sigant had remained silent and did the same. He was a true marvel, having 2 heads might be a liability but they each had a telepathic link with one another and as such learned quicker. That wasn’t to say he did not have brutal encounters where he resorted to his giant-like strength. The years faded quickly as he lived, died and became undead. It was during this time they had met Szass Tam and quickly found an ally and an enemy in one. Tam had taken Eshpel from Bohgil years earlier in a rare game of chance and even though the mage and captain’s relationship allowed them to have a long profitable existence the animosity of that act ate at Tam. Details are sparse but at some point Bohgil and Sigant discovered how Tam had used the artifact to become a lich, the same book he had lost years ago, but kept under strict seals that bound it to its master.

During his years the pair learned of the existence of a sea in the stars and ships that would allow him to sail on them, a Spelljammer. It was then that he plotted and succeeded in stealing Eshpel, taking its power for his own and learning the secrets of lichdom. He was able to secure a ship and used the artifact to power it and left the Crystal Sphere of Faerun to sail the phlogiston and pillage with his crew of pirates across worlds.

Many years passed and the Astral Seas and Wildspace he sailed in seeped into Bohgil and Sigant. They eventually severed their corpse body from their skulls and became demiliches infused with the power of the Astral, the ultimate if lazy, form of undead mage. They would waste no part of them and it was decided that their headless body would be a slave to them on the ship, Willy.  Sigant, now finally free of his brother, became his first mate, their secret still kept even from their crew, and they terrorized the stars with brutal and vicious piracy.

That was until a desperate situation gave Bohgil a choice between his own existence, his crew, and his ship or unbinding the power of Eshpel to use it to escape. Its incredible magical energy shot it across galaxies back to its home on Faerun, of all places. It rocketed into the sky and as it was falling from the heavens so was Eshpel, clasped by Willy, falling all the way down into the lands of Amn with a crash.

What Bohgil does not realize is that in losing Sigant as an attachment to himself he has developed a need for another being to share his mind with. Eshpel fulfilled this role as a slave mind, but constant companion and he is acutely affected by this missing sense of self. This is why he is so desperate to regain it outside of just repairing his ship. It is the first time in many years that Bohgil has had this much passion for one thing and it is reinvigorating and addicting. He was unaware of how much he lost to the laziness of time as he had become a demilich and it has reignited long dormant feelings causing him to be even more dangerous than others of his kind.

Voiceover - The character's voice is strong, deep and eloquent. The character’s dialog tone is arrogant, dominant and uncompromising, even to those who are not on his crew everything is an order. Extremely focused on his own goals without a care for others. His word is law and his motivations are reality and he brooks no interference with those that intervene with his own desires.

### Nephetel - Employed by Szass Tam

Background and Family  
Nephetel, a Tiefling born in Thay, comes from mysterious fiendish lineage. Her mother had two children, each from different fathers. Enegar, her younger brother by three years, is a Bhaalspawn—a child born from Bhaal himself, god of murder. Their shared mother and Enegar’s stepfather (Nephetel’s father) raised them, and they loved both children deeply. Enegar has no relationship with Bhaal beyond the blood he inherited and considers their stepfather his true parent. Nephetel embraced Enegar fully as her brother, protective of him in every sense.

Childhood and Awakening of Abilities  
From a young age, Nephetel discovered her talents as a shadowdancer—these abilities allowed her to slip into the shadows, vanish from sight, and evade capture. These powers fueled her playful, mischievous personality, and she used them in small acts of daring and evasion. Over time, her skills evolved and shaped her shadowdancer abilities, as she grew increasingly agile and elusive.

She also noticed that Enegar faced darker challenges, likely due to the taint of Bhaal in his veins, which plagued him with fits of rage and troubling thoughts. Nephetel became his steadfast protector, defending him against any prejudice for being both a Tiefling and a Bhaalspawn. She encouraged his efforts to control his darker urges and stood by him as he worked to resist them.

Trauma and Survival  
When Nephetel was twelve and Enegar was eight, tragedy struck: a Bhaalist cult raided their settlement. In the chaos, Nephetel’s parents were killed as she bravely tried to shield her brother. Despite her efforts, Enegar was captured, and she was left bruised, alone, and grief-stricken. This event left a deep scar on Nephetel, and only Enegar could draw out her hidden pain and trauma.

Now an orphan, Nephetel had no choice but to survive by any means necessary. She turned to petty theft, pickpocketing, and cunning manipulation to make ends meet. Eventually, her skill and resourcefulness caught the eye of the local Thieves’ Guild, which took her in. Within the guild, she found a kind of surrogate family and was able to refine her shadowdancer abilities further, training in theft, stealth, ambush, and deception.

Life in the Guild and Rising Strength  
As Nephetel grew, her reputation within the Thieves’ Guild flourished, and she eventually caught the attention of the Shadowmaster of the Shadow Thieves. Her exploits earned her favor and access to rare gear and resources, and she continued honing her skills with the guild’s support. Nephetel learned from her life in the shadows that prejudices and others’ judgments held no power over her. Strength and skill became her armor, and she learned to stand confidently against ignorance and fear.

A Hidden Purpose: Working for Szass Tam  
When Szass Tam, the formidable lich and leader of Thay, took notice of her talents, he hired Nephetel for missions that required infiltration, evasion, and subterfuge. These missions were challenging and often morally questionable, yet she accepted them to get closer to Enegar, who she learned was under Tam's influence. Nephetel viewed her employment under Szass Tam as a necessary evil to achieve her ultimate goal: rescuing her brother from the clutches of darkness. She buried her regrets, focusing only on the end goal, though she always preferred less abhorrent paths when possible.

Present Motivations and Personality  
Nephetel has become an expert at hiding her true intentions, using her playful demeanor and sass as a front for her more serious purpose. Her early mischievousness has evolved into a calculated persona, one she uses to mask her pain and single-minded determination. She maintains an air of control, often appearing unfazed or lighthearted, yet beneath it all is a woman carefully weighing each move, aware of the stakes at every turn. Her dedication to rescuing Enegar remains her driving force, and it is only through this mission that she believes she can find peace.

Nephetel’s journey has molded her into someone profoundly resilient and, at times, secretive to a fault. She trusts few and reveals even less, all to protect the one bond that truly matters to her. In her heart, she knows that only by freeing Enegar from the sinister grip of Szass Tam can she hope to lay her pain to rest and perhaps, finally, begin a life on her own terms.
