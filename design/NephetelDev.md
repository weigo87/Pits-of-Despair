# Design Questions

1. ## How did the Bhaalspawn, whether it is one or both, get taken by the Cult?
    

A raid made by cultists, and these cultists had some magical means of detecting Bhaal’s taint and he was an unlucky catch.

2. ## How is Nephetel related to the Bhaalspawn (one or both) and why? 
    

One of them is her younger sibling. She is not a Bhaalspawn, her father was not Bhaal but a mortal someone else. Regardless of different parentage they share the same mother and she treats him as her real full brother and wants to protect him.

3. ## Why does Nephetel want to save the Bhaalspawn (one or both)?
    

Because one of them is a sibling of hers, and she is protective of him.

My gut thinking is to say it doesn’t matter where they are from, it only matters that they are within the reach of Thay, Szass Tam, and the Bhaalist cult. If you have more specific ideas, let me know.

We can certainly explore the matter of their parents a bit and the farm. Perhaps they were poor, and her parents died, which left her to take care of her younger brother.

One way to spin this is that perhaps their parents were murdered by the cult, kidnapped the boy, and while Nephetel tried to get her brother back, she was slapped unconscious and left (or left for dead), which makes for an extremely tragic backstory and fuels her motivations, and may explain why she’s so guarded on a level and the façade of her attitude.

Under it all, she takes things very seriously and is usually a calculating person. She will bend as many rules as necessary to achieve this objective, but will shy off from outright malevolence.

After she bonds with the PC enough and earns enough trust, there’s some hindsight in place which leads to regret, which you can then use to absolve her or to condemn her, which could end the romance (or damage a friendship, if that makes sense too).

6. ## Give a brief summary of Nephetel’s Brother, Enegar
    

Nephetel’s younger brother. He’s also a tiefling, born from the same mother, but a different father, aka Bhaal. He is four years younger than Nephetel.

Before the kidnapping, he suffered the consequences of the taint of Bhaal in various ways:

- Random urges to hurt or kill, similar to sadistic psychopathic children in the real world, as a product of Bhaal’s influence (but not exactly, because it wasn’t felt exactly as a part of him, but as an influence that could manifest without taking full control of him). He once badly injured another boy in the neighborhood after being bullied or shunned due to him being “weird”. After that, he vowed to never lose control, and never did again until the cult took him, which tragically forced him to engage in violence once more in the most depraved way. He might have to be calmed down by Nephetel in various instances, which is part why the bond exists. When Nephetel rescues him, or tries to, she will be invoking the very same thing and try to calm him down, despite him being very far gone comparatively, as he can become Bhaal’s avatar.
- Because of these issues, he displayed behavioral oddities, feelings of isolation, wanting to be alone, and other aspects that would look introverted or autistic, which led to various instances of bullying, which tested his zen. He wanted to be left alone and to be able to suppress those feelings.
- Sudden thoughts of violence when an opportunity arose, or when looking at someone in a submissive or vulnerable state. He had to ask himself why he had those thoughts. Nephetel had to work as his therapist in a way. This all led to rumors from the populace about him being cursed, a pariah, etc. They might have had to defend themselves against a small angry mob in the past.

And it’s the ones she cares about or respects who really matter.

12. ## How does this affect her personality?
    

Hidden under layers of playful secretiveness. She keeps her cool, but the matter of her brother and rescuing him might be the one thing that makes her lose her cool or get otherwise upset or emotional. In most situations unless under duress, she handles it without anyone noticing it.

But her symptoms of distress would be typical human signs of concealed distress or anger, like microexpressions, twitches at the nose or mouth, fleeting smirks of scorn, maybe an odd rolling of the eyes. Since she’s a tiefling, maybe she can become slightly hot to the touch, or have some other infernal trait pop up more. I was going to say “brimstone” but I don’t want her stinking up the place either.

Hot to touch is a good one if the PC reaches out to touch her in a romantic way and mentions she feels “hot blooded” to which she gets annoyed with thinking they are making fun of her Tiefling nature.

She is generally in full control and is not intimidated by anger, she handles everything with a playful charm, but she doesn’t overdo it, when we have to get down to serious business, she will speak in serious business, and only throw some sass. If she expressed anger, she would do it with mockery, sass, and maybe in a ruthless confident manner. The one and only thing that will knock her out of that attitude is her brother and his fate – if this is the case, she will be significantly more emotional and fearful.

In general, this fine control allows her to not take it out on others, although the matter of her brother might cause her to displace it a bit briefly or for as long as she remains distressed. The matter of her brother is the one thing that she has not resolved and made peace with because it’s still within grasp, and not beyond hope. It’s her biggest regret and source of pain. Otherwise she learned to cope and deal with it as best as anyone could imagine. She fenced for herself and survived as a Thief without being outright evil with great resilience, but nothing can wipe that memory and that pain without a successful resolution.

This is where sadness and fear will come in. She will feel happiness and satisfaction when work and tasks are accomplished effectively. She dislikes incompetence and is annoyed by wasting time.

13. ## How open/secretive is Nephetel?
    

Secretive from the outset, but played in a playful (pun not intended) manner. As the relationship deepens with the PC (romance-independently), she will be more open. The cause for this deepening could be depending on choices, or simply as part of the natural progression of the story. The first one is more interesting, in my view, since it involves player agency, RPG, and choices, but is naturally more involved. Examples of choices that may change this:

- Moral choices the player makes
- Responses to her questions or riddles
- Who they side with.
- How they approach something (coldly and logically, or passionately and impulsively, for example)

The mod could keep a global variable or some such to keep track of the progress of this. For example a global variable called NephetelTrust that starts off at 0 and may go into negative or positive depending on choices, and with those numbers we can make branching paths in future events, and dialogs to represent how she views the player.

From the beginning, she is supposed to be playful, but still ironclad as to her true intentions or what she truly thinks. The PC may think that this playfulness means she can be manipulated into spilling the beans, but it simply doesn’t work. She will be more open if she wants to be more open.

Little by little she can become more or less secretive using my proposal implementation.

As for breakpoints in relationship/trust we can say that romance cannot happen if trust is in the negative, and it doesn’t progress if it’s not high enough at some point too. The same rationale would apply to how open she is. We could have variants of responses depending on this level, which may help, help less, or hinder a player that wants to figure out the quest or the plot. The actual numbers used are arbitrary, depending on how many jumps we want, what numbers we want to track, and how many changes are there depending on trust level. So (-10,10) might be good or (-20, 20) might be good, it just depends on how much we flesh out her trust level. Is every number a new state with new changes, or are we going to check for example that if above 15, romance is possible, and no longer check values above that. That’s an implementation detail and it will become more clear as we define a number of moments that build or destroy her trust.
