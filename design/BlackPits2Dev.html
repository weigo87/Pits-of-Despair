The Black Pits 2

https://baldursgate.fandom.com/wiki/The_Black_Pits_II:_Gladiators_of_Thay

        

This mod is split into two parts initially. The first part is the Black Pits 2 in BG2 as it’s just a functional content expansion of Beamdog’s standalone gladiatorial pit. This part adds the Black Pits 2 to the main SoA campaign and the gladiatorial matches (19 in total) and will include reduced experience, gold, and items as well as have the stock of the shop found there adjusted for non-unique items. This is necessary in order to curb the power inflation due to being designed as a standalone that are incorporated into the base BG2 game as well as not lock them out of all other potential mod content or chunks of the game for a long period.

The narrative reasoning for this overall reduction will be explained through the feeling of “being absent from the weave” effect that dampens everything that transpires in the Pits. This ties into the Pits of Despair mod which will be integrated into Pits of Despair as we complete milestones.

Fitting BP2 into the BG2 Campaign

The start of the Black Pits 2 will require 850,000 experience. The PC will receive a summons to Trademeet from Vyantri (See Dialog doc). The summons include the Resplendent Ruby Ring (the BP2 ring with the script effect) for the PC that will start the BP2 campaign when brought to Vyatri’s Pub in Trademeet and entering. The initial cutscene will start.

The party is allowed to escape twice and the completed narrative will be edited in order to allow for it; they will be pulled back in by the Planar Hunters after resting once they hit the proper experience points. Because the PC is trapped it will make sense that outside narratives will not progress while in the Pits.

Fitting BP1 into Pits of Despair

        We will include a modular option for all references to Black Pits 1 to be included in the dialog while the base mod will remove the PCs link to BP1. This will enable the installed component to decide if the PC had completed the Black Pits. If BP_in_BG is installed we will skip this component.

Fitting the Pits of Despair story into BP2

The Black Pits 2 is the route to Szass Tam’s secret lair where he has been sending out his minions to find and get Eshpel through Nephetel and the Cabal. The story advances between defeating Black Pits 2 tiers or progression of the PC to Watcher’s Keep. Dennaton must eventually be dealt with before the final section of the mod story can be accessed. This can be done peacefully or through combat based on the dialog choices and decisions with Nephetel or Bride. Once bypassed there is a significant area-wide assault throughout the entire Black Pits, becoming a mini-dungeon with multiple encounters culminating in a key match before being able to progress. Dennaton is using pages from Eshpel to lure whoever has it to the Black Pits 2 to observe, kill, or manipulate to achieve it.

The gladiatorial fights progression is based on the loot item “Pages from an ancient spell book” to claim pages. The end of each tier rewards Pages from an ancient spell book, for every page combined with Eshpel the PC is allowed a single reward link. For every 2nd reward link the emotional link between PC and Eshpel increases. This progresses the relationship and options to interact with Nephetel and Bride.

 Each tier consists of 5 encounters that are targeted to Chapter 3, 5, 7, and 9 in terms of relative difficulty and enemy strength Here is the experience table for the BG series. https://baldursgate.fandom.com/wiki/Experience_Tables There is an increase in difficulty on the final encounter of each tier. The final match of each tier is a special fight that will allow the escape of the party from the gladiatorial pit. The reasoning ranges from attempting to take the pages from the PC for revenge for specific BP2 storyline points or being hired by one faction or the other to eliminate them once the PC has been revealed to be participating in the matches.

While the PC is progressing through the pits, Nephetel and Bride are working on other methods of eliminating or stealing from the PC. With two opposing factions attempting to gain Esphel they will clash or assist the PC based on dialog choices or relationship triggers with Eshpel. Dialog paths can lead to double crosses, alliances, or attacks. Bride and Nephetel’s primary quest paths will lead to the Black Pits at one point; these are optional quests but completion can lead to changes to the final stages of the mod.

The reasoning for including the BP2 into the ToB campaign becomes revealed in the last tier where the mod will more strongly hint that a Bhaalspawn is in charge of the Pits who seek to remain independent from Denneton and his sponsor Szass Tam in Thay.  

This Bhaalspawn follows the same logic as the Five in the ToB story in that his essence must either be gathered or rejected in order to progress in accepting or rejecting godhood. The Bhaalspawn Cult nature is not like Balthazar or Viekang and instead they have embraced it’s destructive and murderous qualities fully, much like Saeravok, which is why they compete in the gladiatorial pit. They do so in order to lure or capture and kill powerful Bhaalspawn. Each time the PC escapes Dennaton and Szass Tam become agitated.

Szass Tam has a particular interest in the PC competing gladiatorial bouts because he knows the secret of Pits and counts on the Bhaalspawn Cult and the Winged to be bloodthirsty enough to eliminate the PC and take Eshpel from their corpse or from the corpse of any who claim it. However, the dialog options will allow for a non-combat ending for the Bhaalspawn Kastulor where his half-sister Nephetel attempts to save him from the Bhaalspawn Cult.

The Hero and the Villain

        The PC party (or team) can be considered a hero or a villain for that particular match. Being one or or the other changes how much favor and what constitutes earning or losing favor.

        Hero Team - Gain favor for every PC party member healed or resurrected. Gain favor for having matches with Good aligned companions.  Gain favor when showing mercy when the enemy team forfeits the match. Gain favor when a match is against a Villain opponent. Loses favor if against another Hero team.. Lose favor for every dead companion at the end of the match

        Villain Team -  Gains favor for every PC party member that dies. Gain favor for favor for having matches with Evil aligned companions. Gains favor for every enemy team member killed by vorpal effect. Lose favor for every PC party member resurrected.

        No longer have to ask for rewards, they will be automatically given at the end of the match if earned. The voice lines will need to be recut as needed.
